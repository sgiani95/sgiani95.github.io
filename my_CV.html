chemist-turned-safety leader
who blends deep scientific expertise with practical strategies to
make laboratories and working environment safer, smarter, and more sustainable.

I am a Swiss-based chemical safety expert and analytical chemist with over 15 years of experience spanning academia, industry, and public research institutions. I earned a MSc in Molecular and Biological Chemistry from EPFL and a PhD in Physical Chemistry from the University of Zurich, focusing on spectroscopic property calculations of extended systems.

After academic work on quantum chemical methods, I spent over a decade at Mettler-Toledo developing and applying first thermal analysis techniques, publishing on vapor pressure, polymer characterization, and laboratory compliance standards. Second I worked in the field of thermal values (e.g. melting- and boiling point), digital density and refractometry determinations. I later joined Empa as a laboratory safety specialist, advising on chemical and nanotechnology risks, training programs, and emergency preparedness.

Currently Chemical Safety Officer and Dangerous Goods Safety Advisor at Universitätsspital Zürich, I leads chemical safety projects, hazardous goods compliance, and hazardous materials management. My recent research blends chemical education, sustainability, and computational tools.

Fluent in multiple languages, I aim bridging rigorous technical expertise with practical safety leadership and sustainable laboratory practices.
